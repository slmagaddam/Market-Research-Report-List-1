<p><p>Corrosion Inhibiting Admixtures are materials added to concrete to prevent steel reinforcement from corroding. The market is divided into Organic and Inorganic types. Organic inhibitors, such as amines and carboxylates, form a protective film on steel surfaces, blocking corrosive agents. Inorganic inhibitors, like nitrites and phosphates, chemically react with steel to create a passive layer, enhancing durability. Both types improve concrete lifespan, with organic options often preferred for versatility and inorganic for cost-effectiveness in aggressive environments.</p></p>
