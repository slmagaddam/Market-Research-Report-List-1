<p><p>The Chardonnay market is primarily divided into two types: oaked and unoaked. Oaked Chardonnay is characterized by its rich, creamy texture and flavors of vanilla, butter, and toast, resulting from aging in oak barrels. This style is often associated with warmer climates, where ripe fruit flavors prevail. In contrast, unoaked Chardonnay offers a fresher, crisper profile with higher acidity, showcasing citrus and green apple notes. This style appeals to consumers seeking a lighter, more vibrant wine experience, often reflecting cooler growing conditions.</p></p>
