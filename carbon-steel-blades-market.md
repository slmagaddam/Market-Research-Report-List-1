<p><p>The carbon steel blades market is categorized into low-carbon and high-carbon steel types. Low-carbon steel blades, with less than 0.3% carbon, are softer, more ductile, and easier to sharpen but lack edge retention. They are ideal for general-purpose tools and applications requiring flexibility. High-carbon steel blades, with 0.6%-1.5% carbon, are harder, more durable, and retain sharp edges longer, making them suitable for precision cutting tools and heavy-duty applications. Both types balance cost, performance, and usability across various industries.</p></p>
