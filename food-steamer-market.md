<p><p>The food steamer market features two primary types: traditional steamers and electric steamers. Traditional steamers typically consist of stackable pots or bamboo baskets, using stovetop heat or boiling water to generate steam for cooking. They offer a hands-on, versatile approach to steaming various foods. On the other hand, electric steamers are standalone appliances that automate the steaming process, offering precise temperature control and built-in timers, making it convenient for users seeking efficiency and ease in meal preparation. Each type serves distinct culinary needs and preferences.</p></p>
