<p><p>The fire-resistant glass market comprises four main types: laminated, ceramic, tempered, and wired glass. Laminated glass features a plastic interlayer between layers of glass, enhancing safety and fire resistance. Ceramic glass can withstand high temperatures, making it ideal for fire-rated applications. Tempered glass is heat-treated for increased strength and can resist thermal stress, while wired glass incorporates a wire mesh for added security and fire resistance. Each type serves distinct purposes in safety, architecture, and fire protection.</p></p>
