<p><p>The Glass Fiber Complex Materials market encompasses two primary types: thermoset and thermoplastic composites. Thermoset composites, which are created by curing resin that hardens permanently, offer excellent strength and thermal resistance but lack recyclability. In contrast, thermoplastic composites feature resins that can be remelted and reshaped, providing greater flexibility and recyclability, making them ideal for various applications. Both types leverage glass fibers to enhance mechanical properties and are widely used across industries such as automotive, aerospace, and construction.</p></p>
